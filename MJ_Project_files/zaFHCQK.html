Jackson 5's Inception

1963 — 1968

Within these 5 years, the Jackson 5, a musical group consisting of Jackson and his brothers—Jermaine, Tito, Jackie, and Marlon forms and begins to perform at talent shows, gaining popularity and eventually signing a record deal with Motwon Records.

Early Billboard Success

1970

The Jackson 5’s first four singles, “I Want You Back,” “ABC,” “The Love You Save,” and “I’ll Be There,” peak at No. 1 on the Billboard Hot 100. This makes Jackson the youngest artist to reach No. 1.

Michael Jackson Releases His First Two Solo Albums

1972

Jackson’s first two solo albums, Got To Be There and Ben, are released. The latter includes his first No. 1 single, “Ben.”

Jackson's Releases His First Adult Solo Album

1979

Jackson releases his first adult solo album, Off The Wall, and sees immediate success, as it is the highest-selling album of all time by a black artist to date. From this album Jackson notches two more number 1 singles,“Don’t Stop ’Til You Get Enough” and “Rock With You.”

Loading

Jackson Releases Thriller

1982

Jackson releases his second adult solo album, Thriller, which becomes the highest-selling album of all time. He once again registers multiple number 1 singles from Thriller including “Billie Jean” and “Beat It.”

Tragedy and Succes

1984

Tragically, while filming a commercial for Pepsi, Jackson is burned in a pyrotechnic accident and rushed to the hospital. More positively, this year he wins eight Grammy Awards in 10 categories for Thriller

Continued Success

1985

The Making of Michael Jackson’s ‘Thriller’ wins a Grammy for Best Video Album. The charity single “We Are the World” is released and peaks at No. 1. It is estimated to be the highest-selling single of the 1980s.

Loading

BAD

1987

Jackson releases his third adult solo album, Bad, and becomes the first artist to have five No. 1’s from one album: “Bad,” "The Way You Make Me Feel,” “Man in the Mirror,” “I Just Can’t Stop Loving You,” and “Dirty Diana.” Jackson embarks on his first solo tour, which breaks several records of attendance.

Jackson gives a live Interview

1993

Jackson does an Interview with Oprah Winfrey titled, "Michael Jackson Talks...To Oprah" He also recieves the Grammy Legend Award presented by his sister, Janet Jackson. The LA police department begins a criminal inverstigation based on accusations by a 13-year old.

Michael Jackson releases his 5th adult solo album

1995

Jackson's fifth solo album, titled "HIStory: Past, Present, and future book 1, remains the highest-selling double album of all time. His no.1 singles from the disc include, "You Are Not Alone" and "Earth Song".
